Employed full-solvent ab initio molecular dynamics simulations with a slow-growth approach to investigate Ni⁰-Niδ⁺-mediated C−C coupling at partially polarized nickel.

**Key Findings:**
- Conducted AIMD simulations revealing the role of polarization in enhancing CO₂ activation and mitigating poisoning effects of *CO₂ and *CO
- Demonstrated that strategic modulation of Ni's d-band center lowers kinetic barriers for C−C coupling
- Extended findings to other metals (Fe, Rh, Pd, Co, and Ru) for CO₂ electroreduction catalyst design
